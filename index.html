About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Political Science at the University of Florida. My dissertation, Parity & Violence, explains why communal violence clusters locally by focusing on districts approaching population parity between rival groups.

Methodologically, I build district-level datasets, develop parity indices, and fit models ranging from negative binomial GLMs to Bayesian spatial counts. I also create open-source teaching materials in Python and R for spatial and computational social science.

Research

Selected projects in conflict, secession, and spatial analysis.

Parity & Violence

Subnational dynamics of communal conflict in Iraq (2003–2009), modeling how approaching ethnic parity interacts with political shocks to catalyze mobilization.

  • Methods: spatial GLMs, random effects, robust clustering
  • Tools: R (glmmTMB), ArcGIS Pro, tmap

DDR Sequencing

Markov chain models of Disarmament, Demobilization, and Reintegration using DDR-40 (1980–2020) to estimate transition probabilities and conflict recurrence risk.

  • Methods: Markov chains, survival analysis
  • Tools: R/Python, tidyverse, lifelines

Self-Determination Movements

Multi-country dataset linking referendum outcomes, leadership attributes, and escalation from nonviolent claims to armed conflict; early-warning through ML classifiers.

  • Methods: Random Forests, logistic regression
  • Tools: sklearn, caret, modelsummary
